## Research

I am a member of the [DeMille group](https://demillegroup.psd.uchicago.edu/) in the Department of Physics at University of Chicago. We are a group of experimental atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) physicists who are broadly interested in using cold and ultracold molecules for applications in quantum science and precision probes of physics beyond the Standard Model.

I currently work on the [strontium monofluoride](https://demillegroup.psd.uchicago.edu/research/laser-cooling-and-trapping-of-srf-molecules-towards-a-quantum-gas-of-polar-molecules/) (SrF) direct laser cooling experiment. In this experiment, we start with a cryogenic buffer gas beam source of SrF molecules at 4 K. Using laser slowing, we load roughly 10000 molecules into a magneto-optical trap (MOT). After further laser cooling, we can trap roughly 100 molecules in an optical dipole trap (ODT) at a temperature of roughly 10 uK. This serves as a starting point for further improvements towards achieving a quantum degenerate gas of polar bosonic molecules, or for other studies of an ultracold bulk gas of polar molecules.

Previously, during my undergraduate studies, I worked on research projects in fluid dynamics (self-similarity in turbulent boundary layer flow), computational astrophysics (dark matter halo growth), computational soft matter physics (polymer crystallization), and experimental cosmology (Atacama Cosmology Telescope and Simons Observatory). I entered the field of experimental AMO physics in Fall 2019, when I did my bachelor's thesis with the [Bakr Group](https://ultracold.princeton.edu/) at Princeton University on assembly of ultracold bialkali sodium-rubidium (NaRb) molecules from laser-cooled Na and Rb atoms.

See Miscellaneous Writings for more details of my previous research projects.
