## Research

I am a member of the [DeMille group](https://demillegroup.psd.uchicago.edu/) in the Department of Physics at UChicago. We are a group of experimental atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) physicists who are broadly interested in using cold/ultracold molecules for applications in quantum science and precision probes of physics beyond the Standard Model.

I currently work on the strontium monofluoride (SrF) direct laser cooling experiment. In this experiment, we start with a cryogenic buffer gas beam source of SrF molecules at 4 K. Using laser slowing, we load roughly 10000 molecules into a magneto-optical trap (MOT). After further sub-Doppler cooling, we can trap roughly 100 molecules in an optical dipole trap (ODT) at a temperature of roughly 10 uK. This serves as a starting point for further improvements towards achieving a quantum degenerate gas of polar bosonic molecules, or for other studies of an ultracold bulk gas of polar molecules.
